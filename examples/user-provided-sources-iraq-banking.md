## Mechanism

USD clearing for Iraqi commercial banks runs through the CBI Iraq's dollar auction, which is in turn dependent on:
- CBI Iraq's account at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York (which has been subject to compliance scrutiny and periodic restrictions on Iraqi banks since the early 2020s);
- US Treasury and OFAC actions on specific Iraqi banks identified as facilitating Iran-linked or other sanctioned flows;
- CBI Iraq's own KYC and platform-modernization reforms intended to bring auction participation into compliance with US Treasury expectations.

A correspondent bank's exposure to an Iraqi respondent transmits through:
- the respondent's eligibility for the CBI Iraq USD auction (the operational lifeline for USD activity);
- the respondent's specific OFAC and NY Fed status;
- transactional exposure to flows that may be Iran-linked or otherwise sanctioned;
- reputational and enforcement risk if the respondent is later named.

## Actor incentives and leverage

- **CBI Iraq:** wants to retain the NY Fed dollar account and minimize US Treasury restrictions; strong incentive to enforce auction compliance, but constrained by domestic political pressure and Iran-linked actors in the Iraqi banking system.
- **US Treasury / OFAC:** wants to constrain Iran-linked dollar flows through Iraq without collapsing the Iraqi financial system; calibrated pressure pattern with periodic named bank restrictions.
- **NY Fed:** operates the CBI Iraq dollar account; willing to impose individual-bank restrictions but not a full account suspension.
- **Iraqi respondent banks:** range from compliant institutions investing in remediation to banks with documented Iran-linked exposure. The distinction matters operationally.
- **Iranian commercial network actors:** strong incentive to maintain Iraqi banking access for sanctions evasion; transmission via specific named banks documented in OFAC actions.
- **Western correspondent bank:** wants to retain trade-finance and remittance business with Iraqi respondents; sanctions enforcement and reputational risk creates strong incentive for selectivity and ongoing monitoring.

## Limitation note

This example is a `user-provided sources` template. Operational compliance decisions require:
- primary verification of current OFAC, EU, UK sanctions designations;
- current NY Fed correspondent guidance;
- current CBI Iraq auction eligibility;

This skill does not perform sanctions screening, AML monitoring, or correspondent-banking due diligence. Use this template as a structural aid for analyst-style reasoning over a user-provided source pack.
