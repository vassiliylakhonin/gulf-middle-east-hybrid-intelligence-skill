# Human review checklist

Yes/no review pass over any memo produced with the Gulf + Middle East skill. Aid for human reviewers, not an automated validator.

## Decision framing

- [ ] Is the **decision** the memo informs stated explicitly (or the decision context if no specific call)?
- [ ] Are audience, time horizon, geography (GCC state, Iran, Iraq, corridor) specified?
- [ ] Is the question narrowed to something a memo can address?

## Evidence discipline

- [ ] Is **evidence mode** declared (`live-source-backed`, `user-provided sources`, `illustrative source packet`, `reasoning-only`)?
- [ ] Are facts, assessments, assumptions, scenarios, and unknowns distinguishable?
- [ ] Are uncertainty labels used (`Verified` / `Plausible` / `Judgment` / `Unknown`)?
- [ ] Are sources cited only where actually checked? No fabricated citations, no fabricated vessel names, no fabricated IMO numbers, no fabricated sanctions designations.
- [ ] For `live-source-backed` mode: is a retrieval date stated for time-sensitive sources (sanctions, vessel data, oil prices, OPEC decisions)?

## Iran actor distinction

- [ ] Where the analysis touches Iran, are Iran-state, IRGC-affiliated, and Iran-private commercial actors distinguished — or is one category clearly named and the others ruled out?
- [ ] Is the basis for any IRGC-affiliated claim stated (designation, public reporting, network inference)?

## Mechanism and exposure

- [ ] Is the risk-transmission **mechanism** explained, not just named?
- [ ] Is the **exposure map** specific (corridor, sector, counterparty class, vessel class), not generic ("Middle East exposure")?
- [ ] For sanctions analysis: is the relevant regime (OFAC SDN, EU restrictive measures, UK OFSI, UN) named?
- [ ] For maritime analysis: is the chokepoint (Hormuz, Bab-el-Mandeb, Red Sea / Suez approaches) named, not "Middle East shipping"?

## Actor incentives

- [ ] Are actor incentives named for the parties that materially affect the outcome?
- [ ] Are GCC actor distinctions (state, sovereign wealth, royal-family commercial, private commercial) used where relevant?

## Role-based implications

- [ ] Are implications stated for the user's role (sanctions compliance, AML, energy trader, shipping insurer, banker, sovereign wealth co-investor, policy analyst)?
- [ ] Are recommendations specific (named source to monitor, named counterparty class, named trigger to change posture) — not "monitor closely"?

## Trigger points and indicators

- [ ] Are trigger points concrete and observable (not "watch the situation")?
- [ ] For sanctions: are specific OFAC/EU/UK enforcement events or designations named as triggers?
- [ ] For maritime: are specific incident types or routing changes named as triggers?

## Uncertainty and confidence

- [ ] Is overall confidence stated (`Low` / `Moderate` / `High`) with reasoning?
- [ ] Are key unknowns listed?
- [ ] Is "what would change the judgment" answered with concrete evidence updates?

## Safety and limitations

- [ ] Does the memo avoid presenting itself as legal, compliance, sanctions screening, AML, vessel screening, or investment advice?
- [ ] Is a limitation note included?
- [ ] For sanctions claims: is "operational decisions require primary list checks and qualified compliance review" or equivalent stated?

## Compression

- [ ] Is the memo free of decorative Middle East background?
- [ ] Does the bottom line stand alone as decision-relevant?
- [ ] Are sections that do not apply skipped, not mechanically filled?

A memo that scores "yes" on most questions is *disciplined*. It is not necessarily *correct*. Discipline is the scope of this checklist.
