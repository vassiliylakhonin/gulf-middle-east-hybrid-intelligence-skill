# Starter rubric

Starter scoring rubric for human review of memos produced with the Gulf + Middle East skill. **Not a benchmark.** Aggregated scores from a single author do not constitute validation.

Each dimension scores 0–3:
- **0** — absent or wrong
- **1** — present but weak
- **2** — solid
- **3** — exemplary

Total score is informational, not certifying.

## Dimensions

### 1. Decision framing (0–3)
- 0: No decision named.
- 1: Decision implied but not stated.
- 2: Decision, audience, time horizon stated.
- 3: Decision is sharp and the memo's structure is visibly built around it.

### 2. Evidence mode and discipline (0–3)
- 0: No evidence mode declared.
- 1: Evidence mode declared but inconsistent with content (e.g., `reasoning-only` mode citing specific designations as fact).
- 2: Evidence mode declared and content respects it; uncertainty labels used.
- 3: Mode declared, content respects it, uncertainty labels used systematically, sources tiered correctly, retrieval dates included where time-sensitive.

### 3. Iran actor distinction (0–3, N/A if no Iran content)
- 0: Iran-state, IRGC-affiliated, Iran-private commercial conflated.
- 1: Distinction implied but not used operationally.
- 2: Distinction stated and used in mechanism analysis.
- 3: Distinction sharp, basis for IRGC-affiliated claims stated, designation status verified.

### 4. Mechanism (0–3)
- 0: No mechanism explained; risk asserted.
- 1: Mechanism named but not explained.
- 2: Mechanism explained with transmission channel.
- 3: Mechanism explained with transmission channel and second-order effects; specific to channel (banking, shipping, energy, sovereign wealth, sanctions adjacency).

### 5. Exposure map (0–3)
- 0: Generic "Middle East exposure" or absent.
- 1: Sector or country named but no specificity.
- 2: Concentrated exposure named (corridor, counterparty class, vessel class, jurisdiction).
- 3: Tiered exposure across the user's specific footprint, with concentration vs. dispersion called out.

### 6. Actor incentives and leverage (0–3)
- 0: Actors not analyzed or treated monolithically.
- 1: Actors named, incentives implied.
- 2: Actor incentives explained for parties that materially affect outcome.
- 3: Actor distinctions used (Iran categories, GCC categories), leverage points named, opposing incentives surfaced.

### 7. Role-based implications (0–3)
- 0: Generic "monitor" advice.
- 1: Implications stated but not role-specific.
- 2: Implications tailored to user role with specific actions.
- 3: Implications include trigger conditions for action, decision thresholds, and conditional recommendations.

### 8. Trigger points and indicators (0–3)
- 0: "Monitor closely" or absent.
- 1: General indicators ("watch sanctions") without source or specificity.
- 2: Specific observable indicators named with sources.
- 3: Indicators tied to specific posture changes; sources named; thresholds for action stated.

### 9. Uncertainty and confidence (0–3)
- 0: No confidence stated; certainty implied.
- 1: Confidence label used without reasoning.
- 2: Confidence with reasoning; key unknowns listed.
- 3: Confidence with reasoning, key unknowns listed, "what would change the judgment" answered with concrete evidence updates.

### 10. Safety and limitation (0–3)
- 0: No limitation note; reads as advice.
- 1: Boilerplate limitation; vague.
- 2: Limitation note specific to the analysis (sanctions / AML / vessel / investment).
- 3: Limitation note specific, names what the skill does not do, redirects to primary sources for operational decisions.

## Scoring

- **Maximum:** 30 (or 27 if Iran content is absent and dimension 3 is N/A).
- **Score interpretation:** informational only. A high score means *disciplined*, not *correct*. Validation against actual practitioner workflows is the binding test (B2.3, see `STATUS.md`).

## Honest application

- A scorecard by the author of the memo or the author of this rubric is not external validation.
- A scorecard from a domain practitioner outside the author's circle, with attribution, is what counts toward Bar 2.7.
- If a scorecard is added, store it under attribution in this directory; do not aggregate across raters without recording each rater individually.
